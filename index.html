About Me

I work at the intersection of computational neuroimaging, medical image analysis, and machine learning. My research centers on developing methods for brain image processing, connectivity analysis, white matter modeling, and multimodal neuroimaging.

A major goal of my work is to improve the robustness, interpretability, and practical value of neuroimaging methods. I am particularly interested in how computational models can help characterize brain structure across different spatial scales and support more sensitive and reliable imaging-based analysis.

I am also interested in AI-driven approaches for medical imaging and in building tools that support reproducible, data-driven neuroscience research.

Research Overview

Brain Connectivity and White Matter Organization

I study computational approaches for characterizing brain connectivity and white matter structure, with an emphasis on methods that are robust, interpretable, and biologically meaningful.

Quantitative and Multimodal Neuroimaging

My research also explores how quantitative imaging measures from multiple modalities can be integrated to better understand brain structure and variation.

AI for Medical Imaging

I am interested in AI-driven methods that support image analysis, modeling, and reproducible research workflows in medical imaging.
