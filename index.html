About

I work at the intersection of computational neuroimaging, medical image analysis, and machine learning. My research centers on developing methods for brain image processing, connectivity analysis, white matter modeling, and multimodal neuroimaging.

A major goal of my work is to improve the robustness, interpretability, and practical value of neuroimaging methods — particularly how computational models can characterize brain structure across spatial scales and support more sensitive, reliable imaging-based analysis.

I am also interested in AI-driven approaches for medical imaging and in building tools that support reproducible, data-driven neuroscience research.
